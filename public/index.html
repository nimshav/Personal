Ph.D. in Communication | Lecturer, Editor, and Mentor

About Me

I am a communication scholar, lecturer, and mentor with extensive academic experience in both teaching and research. My work focuses on the intersections of language, society, and cultural discourse, examining how communication practices shape public and private spheres.

I hold a Ph.D. from the University of Massachusetts Amherst and have taught at institutions such as the University of Haifa, Netanya Academic College, and Stetson University. Additionally, I provide editorial and mentorship services to graduate students and academically employed scholars.
